Four things you can do to help climate change:

Reduce Energy Usage

Lower your energy usage by using energy-efficient appliances, turning off lights when not in use, and adjusting your thermostat. Switch to LED bulbs and unplug devices when theyâ€™re not needed. Small changes in energy consumption can significantly reduce greenhouse gas emissions over time.

Choose Sustainable Transportation

Walk, bike, carpool, or use public transit whenever possible. If you drive, consider switching to an electric or hybrid vehicle. Reducing reliance on fossil fuel-powered cars cuts down on carbon emissions and helps improve air quality in your community.

Minimise Waste

Reduce, reuse, and recycle to limit the amount of waste sent to landfills. Compost food scraps, avoid single-use plastics, and opt for products with minimal packaging. Landfills produce methane, a potent greenhouse gas, so reducing waste directly helps combat climate change.

Support Renewable Energy

If possible, switch to renewable energy sources like solar or wind power for your home. Many utility companies offer green energy options. Advocating for and supporting policies that promote renewable energy can also accelerate the transition away from fossil fuels.
